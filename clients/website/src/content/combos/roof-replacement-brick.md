## The spec changes with the section

Brick went up fast in the 1950s–70s, and most of those roofs are now finishing their second or third shingle cycle. But an inland Laurelton cape and an elevated Baywood lagoon house are not the same job, and we don't price them like they are. Every [roof replacement](/roof-replacement/) we build in Brick includes:

- **Full tear-off** — never a layover on 60-year-old decking
- Deck inspection with plank or plywood swapped on the spot (routine on the older Herbertsville stock)
- Ice & water shield at eaves and valleys, synthetic underlayment across the field
- **Aluminum drip edge and flashing** anywhere near the lagoons or the Metedeconk — galvanized steel rusts out years early in salt air
- 130 mph architectural shingles with six-nail fastening within sight of open water, from Shore Acres out to Normandy Beach
- Ridge venting sized to your attic's actual intake
