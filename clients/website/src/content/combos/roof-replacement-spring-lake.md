## A replacement built for a three-block-from-the-Atlantic Victorian

A roof replacement in Spring Lake typically runs $12,000–$28,000, and the reason it sits above the Shore average is simple: the homes are bigger and the rooflines are harder. Instead of a 17-square ranch, you are usually looking at 30 to 50 squares spread across six or eight planes, with valleys, dormers, turret courses, and porch-roof transitions that punish a shortcut. We price and build for that complexity, and we spec every roof in town for the salt air and nor'easters it will actually face.

## The spec we hold to, oceanfront to lakefront

Whether it's an Ocean Avenue oceanfront Victorian, a Divine Park lakefront home, or a newer custom rebuild in the North End, the build sheet is the same coastal baseline:

- **Full tear-off** to the deck — no laying new shingles over old, so we can see and fix what's underneath
- **Deck inspection** with soft or rotted sheathing replaced before anything covers it
- **Ice & water shield** at eaves, valleys, and every penetration; synthetic underlayment across the full deck
- **130 mph wind-rated architectural shingles**, six-nail fastening, sealed starter and edge courses
- **Corrosion-resistant flashing and fasteners** chosen for salt exposure — bargain metal rusts here years before shingles wear out
- **Ridge venting** matched to intake so summer attic heat doesn't cook the roof from below

## How the day runs

Most Spring Lake homes come off and go back on in a single day, even the complex ones — tear-off starts early, the deck gets inspected and repaired, underlayment and shingles go down, and cleanup finishes before dinner. Genuinely large or intricate Victorians occasionally run into a second day, and we tell you that up front, not mid-job. Because you can't judge a three-story roof from the sidewalk, we document the finished ridges, valleys, and flashing by drone so you inspect the work from your porch.
