## How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Jackson?

Most kitchen remodels in Jackson run **$25,000–$90,000**. A ranch or cape in Cassville or Jackson Mills getting new cabinet fronts, counters, and lighting sits toward the low end; a full gut-and-reconfigure in one of the township's 1990s–2000s colonials — moved plumbing, an island, custom cabinetry — reaches the top. Every estimate is itemized by scope, not a guess.

## Kitchens built for Jackson's big colonials

Jackson grew fast between the mid-1990s and 2008, and thousands of those colonials on former farmland shipped with a builder-grade kitchen: stock cabinets, laminate counters, a galley layout that never quite worked. Twenty-plus years later, those are the kitchens we gut most often. Because these houses tend to sit on generous lots with room to spare inside too, we can often open a wall toward the family room, add an island, and turn a closed-off kitchen into the room the whole house actually uses. As your [kitchen remodel](/kitchen-remodel/) general contractor, Paragon manages the layout change, the licensed trades, and the permit with Jackson Township in one project.

## Right-sized kitchens for Four Seasons and Westlake

Jackson's two large 55+ communities, Four Seasons at Metedeconk Lakes and Westlake, are reaching the age where original kitchens need real attention. For these homeowners the priorities shift: single-level convenience, lower and more accessible counter runs, brighter task lighting, and easier-reach storage matter more than square footage. We coordinate any HOA design approvals those communities require so the process stays simple.

## Do I need a permit to remodel my kitchen in Jackson Township?

Most kitchen remodels do, because moving or adding plumbing, electrical, or gas lines — which the majority of full remodels involve — triggers a permit and inspection requirement from the Jackson Township building department. Paragon prepares and files that paperwork and schedules every inspection as your general contractor, so you're never the one tracking down forms or waiting on a callback.
