## The symptoms we get called for here

- **A ceiling or wall stain** after a nor'easter drove rain sideways across the river side
- **Rust streaks below a chimney or dormer** — step and counter flashing giving up in salt air
- **Granules in the gutters and bald patches** on a sun-and-wind-beaten southern slope
- **A slipped or missing ridge cap or shingle** after a big blow off the water
- **A drip that only shows in wind-driven rain**, not a steady downpour — almost always a flashing or valley problem, not a field leak

## How we find the real source

Water travels before it drips, so the stain on your ceiling is rarely under the hole. We work the roof from the suspect area outward — flashing at every wall, chimney, and dormer, valley metal, pipe boots, ridge, and nail pops — and on estate roofs with staging we fly **drone footage** so you see the failure without climbing. Elevated post-Sandy homes along the Navesink and in Black Point catch more wind than they did at their old height, and we check the edges and ridges that extra uplift punishes first.

## Repair or replace — the straight answer

We do both, so we have no reason to push you either way. Here's the honest line:

| Your roof | Usually the right call |
|---|---|
| Under ~15 yrs, one isolated leak | Repair — a good fix buys years |
| Localized storm or flashing damage | Repair the metal, keep the roof |
| 20+ yrs, broad granule loss, repeat leaks | Replace — repairs stop paying back |
| Failed builder-grade flashing, sound shingles | Re-flash in stainless, don't re-roof |
