## How Barnegat roofs fail, by age

Three generations of housing in this town, three distinct failure patterns:

- **Village two-stories off East Bay Avenue (1890s–1930s).** Chimney flashing that's been caulked instead of fabricated, and plank decking that flexes with the seasons until old nail holes start to weep. These are the leaks that have "been there for years."
- **Ocean Acres and Pinewood Estates (1970s–90s).** Pine needles pack gutters into dams that push water back under the shingle edge, moss claims the shaded north slopes, and brittle second-cycle shingles crack when a branch so much as bounces off them.
- **Heritage Point, Mirage, Brighton, Pheasant Run (1998–2008).** These homes fail on schedule: pipe boots split around year 15–20, nail pops open up, and builder-grade ridge vents admit sideways nor'easter rain — the stain that only appears during a storm.

## Diagnosis before dollar signs

The stain on your ceiling is rarely under the hole in your roof — water runs the rafters first. So we start in the attic, follow the moisture line back to its entry point, and confirm it topside before naming a price. Everything we find gets photographed, drone included when it tells the story better, and the quote lists each fix as its own line. You approve items, not a lump sum, and most repairs close out in that same visit.
