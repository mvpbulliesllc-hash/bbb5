## Roof repair in Holmdel, straight answers

A sound Holmdel roof leaking in one spot almost always needs a **repair, not a replacement** — most fixes here run **$450 to $1,800** and target flashing, a valley, or wind-lifted shingles rather than the field of the roof. On Holmdel's shaded, tree-lined lots we trace more leaks to failed step flashing at dormers and chimneys and to oak-debris-packed valleys than to the shingles themselves. Paragon Exteriors (NJ HIC #13VH13814500) diagnoses the actual source before quoting, and gets an emergency tarp on fast when water is already inside.

## The symptoms Holmdel homeowners call about

- **Ceiling or wall stains** after a nor'easter or a summer squall line — usually flashing, not a "bad shingle"
- **Drips near a chimney or skylight** — the counter and step flashing on 80s and 90s customs is where these roofs give out first
- **Shingles in the yard** after wind — Crawford Hill and the higher ridgeline lots catch real gusts despite the inland label
- **A limb through the roof** — common on wooded Pleasant Valley and Holmdel Park–adjacent lots, and it often cracks the deck below
- **Granules in the gutters plus black algae streaks** on north slopes — aging, not always leaking, but worth a look

## How we diagnose it

We don't guess from the ground. We get on the roof, open the suspect area, and follow the water to its real entry point — the stain on your ceiling is often feet away from the actual breach, running down a rafter first. We check the flashing, the valleys, the pipe boots, and the deck underneath, then show you photos of what we found. For storm and limb damage we [document everything with drone imagery](/projects/) so your [insurance claim](/blog/roof-insurance-claim-nj/) is built on evidence, not a description.

## When we tell you to repair — and when not to

Honesty is the whole point. A tight, mid-life roof with one failed flashing joint gets repaired, warrantied, and left alone. But if we open it up and find granule loss across whole slopes, brittle shingles, or a leak that's already been "fixed" twice, we'll say so — throwing money at a worn-out roof is the most expensive thing you can do. That's the [repair-versus-replacement call](/blog/roof-repair-vs-replacement/), and when it lands on replace we handle [Holmdel roof replacement](/roof-replacement/holmdel/) too, with [financing](/financing/) if the timing's tight.
