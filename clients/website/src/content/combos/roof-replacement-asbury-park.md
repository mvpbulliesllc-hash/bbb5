## The spec we build for this coastline

A roof replacement in Asbury Park typically costs $8,500–$18,000 for a single-family home and $8–$16 per square foot for flat or low-slope sections, includes a full tear-off to the deck, and — on most single-family houses — finishes in one day. What separates a roof that lasts here from one that curls in ten years is the coastal spec: because every roof in town sits within about a mile of the surf, we build to a 130 mph wind rating as standard, not as an add-on.

That means six nails per shingle instead of four, corrosion-resistant fasteners that salt air can't eat, ice & water shield at eaves and valleys, synthetic underlayment across the whole deck, sealed edge metal, and new flashing at every chimney and wall — the brick chimneys on Sunset Lake Victorians are where old flashing fails first.

## How the job runs, block by block

Asbury Park roofs don't come in one shape, so the process flexes:

- **Sunset Lake Victorians and foursquares** — three stories, steep and cut-up, on tight lots. These are hand tear-offs with careful staging; we protect neighboring property lines and often find plank decking that needs plywood repair before shingles go down.
- **West Side two-families** — usually a clean one-day tear-off and reroof, with attention to the low-slope porch and addition roofs that hang off the back.
- **Downtown mixed-use** — flat brick buildings priced by the square foot in TPO or EPDM membrane, with drainage corrected so ponding after a nor'easter stops finding the seam.
- **Waterfront condos and townhomes** — post-2005 stock now hitting its first real maintenance cycle; full reroofs staged around occupancy.

Every job ends the same way: magnetic nail sweep of the lot and sidewalk, same-day debris haul, manufacturer warranty registered for you, and a drone record of the work.
