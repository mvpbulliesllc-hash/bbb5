No house in this borough sits more than a few blocks from salt water, so the question is never *whether* to build coastal — it's what coastal means in materials:

## Tear-off day on a narrow island lot

Seaside Heights houses sit tight — to each other and to the street — and the 1920s–'50s bungalows between the Ortley Beach line and the Seaside Park border often carry two or three shingle layers. All of it comes off. We tarp your neighbors' siding and gardens on both sides, inspect the deck (decades of salt-laden humidity rot plywood, and replacement sheets are already priced per-sheet in your estimate), then dry-in and shingle to the spec above. Post-Sandy elevated homes on pilings get proper staging, not ladders and optimism. The day ends with a magnetic nail sweep of your lot *and* the neighbors' side strips, plus drone photos of the finished roof.
