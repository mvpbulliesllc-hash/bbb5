## Downtown Victorians vs. Township colonials: two different kitchen jobs

| Where you live | Typical kitchen problem | What the remodel usually involves |
|---|---|---|
| Downtown Freehold (Borough) | Cramped early-1900s galley layout, knob-and-tube remnants, old galvanized supply lines | Rewiring, replumbing, and reworking the footprint within the original structure |
| East Freehold | Closed-off 1960s–70s layout, dated cabinets and laminate counters | Opening a wall to a dining room, new cabinetry, updated electrical panel |
| West Freehold | 1980s–2000s colonial with a workable footprint but tired finishes | Cabinet, counter, and lighting refresh without moving plumbing |
| Georgia & Smithburg | Farmhouse kitchens on rural lots, older well/septic-era plumbing | Full re-plumb alongside the remodel, sometimes coordinated with well pressure checks |

A home near the Monmouth County Courthouse downtown and a colonial off Route 537 near Monmouth Battlefield State Park can carry the same square footage and land at very different price points once we see what's behind the walls.

## Who pulls the permits — Freehold Borough or Freehold Township?

Freehold has a Borough and a Township, depending on your address — we confirm and pull the permits either way. Moving plumbing or gas lines, adding circuits, or opening a structural wall all require sign-off, and each side of town has its own construction office and inspection schedule. We handle the filing and the inspections so you're not the one tracking down paperwork mid-project.
