## How much does a full home renovation cost in Point Pleasant?

Most full home renovations in Point Pleasant run $75,000 to $400,000 or more, depending on square footage, structural scope, and whether exterior work is included. A whole-house interior refresh on an existing cape or ranch footprint sits toward the lower end; a complete renovation of a waterfront or second home — new systems, new layout, new exterior — climbs from there. We walk the entire property in person and hand you a free, itemized estimate before any work begins.

## Coordinating shore homes and second homes

A meaningful share of full renovation work in Point Pleasant is on shore or second homes — properties near Bay Head Shores, the Point Pleasant Canal, and the Manasquan riverfront that owners are bringing up to full-time living standards or preparing for resale. These projects come with their own rhythm: coordinating access when the owner isn't local, scheduling around the shore season, and in some cases working within flood-zone or elevated-construction rules that don't apply to the capes and ranches further inland in West Point Pleasant and Lovelandtown.

## One general contractor, exterior and interior

Full renovations rarely stay confined to the inside of the house. Siding, roofing, and windows often need attention at the same time as kitchens, bathrooms, and structural changes, and running those as two separate contracts usually means two schedules that don't talk to each other. As your general contractor, we manage every trade — interior and exterior — under one contract, so decisions made in the kitchen don't get undone by a window contractor working from a different set of plans.

## Do I need permits for a full home renovation in Point Pleasant?

A renovation of this scale almost always requires multiple permits from Point Pleasant Borough — structural, plumbing, electrical, and exterior — each with its own inspection. (Point Pleasant Beach is a separate municipality, so we verify the correct building department before filing anything.) We manage the entire permit package ourselves, which is typically the most time-consuming part of a project this size if you're not used to navigating it.
