## What actually breaks on a Jackson roof

A hundred square miles of oaks and pitch pines produce a very specific damage list:

- **Limb punctures** — nearly every summer thunderstorm that crosses Route 537 drops a branch on somebody's shingles
- **Packed valleys** — leaves and needles hold moisture against the mat until water finds its way under
- **Moss and black algae** on shaded north slopes, eating shingle life from above
- **Ice dam damage** at eaves the winter sun never reaches — the leak-that-only-happens-in-January problem ([full explainer here](/blog/ice-dams-winter-roof-problems-nj/))
- **Blown tabs and nail pops** on the builder-grade shingles installed across the 1995–2008 developments, now 20+ years old
- **Tired chimney and step flashing** on the postwar capes and ranches around Cassville, Legler, and Jackson Mills

## Diagnosis before dollar one

Water travels. The stain in your hallway ceiling can start at an entry point ten feet upslope, and a patch slapped over the stain fixes nothing. We trace the leak from the attic side and the roof side, photograph what we find — drone shots included — and hand you an itemized quote for the actual problem. You see exactly what we saw before you approve anything.
