## The spec we build for a wooded Colts Neck lot

The enemies here aren't salt air — they're oak canopy and freeze-thaw winters. So we spec accordingly:

- **Full tear-off to the deck** — no layovers hiding tired sheathing on the estate corridors off Route 537 and Route 34
- **Deck inspection and repair** — plank sheathing and layered shingles are common in Scobeyville, Vanderburg, and the older crossroads sections; we price bad decking up front, not mid-job
- **Ice & water shield** run generously at eaves and valleys, because ice dams form over unheated garage wings and cathedral great rooms every winter
- **Algae-resistant architectural shingles** so shaded north slopes don't streak black in five years
- **New flashing** at every chimney, skylight, and wall — the penetrations behind oversized chimneys are where these roofs leak first
- **Attic-matched ridge venting** and heavy-duty [gutter protection](/gutters/) built to survive an oak
