## How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Toms River?

Most Toms River kitchen remodels land between $25,000 and $90,000, depending on scope. A cabinet and counter refresh on an existing layout sits at the low end; a full gut that moves plumbing, reworks electrical, and adds custom cabinetry runs toward the top. We walk your kitchen in person and hand you a free, itemized estimate before any demo starts, so the price you're quoted is the price you pay.

## One general contractor, every trade

Toms River's kitchens tell you what decade they're from. The closed-off, galley-style kitchens common in the 1960s–80s ranches and capes around Holiday City and North Dover were built for a different way of cooking than most families use today. Waterfront and post-Sandy elevated rebuilds in Silverton and along the Barnegat Bay side of town often already have open framing that makes an open-concept layout change more straightforward. Either way, as your general contractor we manage the plumber, electrician, and any gas line work under one roof — you're not hiring three separate contractors and hoping their schedules line up.

## Do I need permits for a kitchen remodel in Toms River?

Most kitchen remodels touch plumbing, electrical, or both, and Toms River Township requires permits any time those systems change — a moved sink, a new gas line for a range, relocated outlets. We pull every permit ourselves and coordinate the inspections, so you're not stuck tracking down paperwork for three different trades. It's one of the reasons homeowners hire a single general contractor instead of managing subs directly.
