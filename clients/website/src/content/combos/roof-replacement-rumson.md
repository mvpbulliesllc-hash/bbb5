## The Rumson spec, line by line

Half of what fails on a Rumson roof isn't the shingle — it's the metal underneath it. So we don't quote a shingle field and wave at the rest:

- **Full tear-off** to the deck, no layovers hiding old damage
- **Deck inspection** with rotted plywood replaced before anything covers it
- **Ice & water shield** at eaves, valleys, and every penetration
- **Synthetic underlayment** across the whole deck — not felt
- **New flashing in coated or stainless** at chimneys, walls, dormers, and every valley — the corrosion points on a peninsula home
- **130 mph architectural shingles**, six-nail fastening, sealed starter and rake edges for wind-driven rain

On the Rumson Road and Bellevue Avenue estates — cedar, slate, turrets, chimney after chimney — the flashing and valley work is the whole job, and it's where a cheap crew gets exposed from the street.
