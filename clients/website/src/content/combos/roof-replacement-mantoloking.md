## From drone scope to final inspection

Every job starts with a drone survey and an itemized written scope — no allowances, no "we'll see when we're up there." We file the permit with the borough construction office at Borough Hall on Downer Avenue (or with Brick Township if you're in South Mantoloking — we confirm which before filing), plan material drops and dumpster placement for the borough's tight lots before the crew crosses the Mantoloking Bridge, and replace any soft decking at a per-sheet price you saw up front. Asphalt roofs wrap in a day; gambrel estates with stacked dormers take two to three, quoted honestly. The day ends with a magnetic nail sweep and drone photos of the finished roof.

Because most owners here are somewhere else for much of the year, the whole project runs remotely if you want it to: approve the scope from the photos, track the work from the drone documentation, inspect the result without driving down. Plenty of Mantoloking roofs go on between October and May for exactly that reason — our take on timing is in [the best time to replace a roof in NJ](/blog/best-time-to-replace-roof-nj/).
