## What we see most in Neptune

- **Wind-lifted and missing shingles** after nor'easters — worst on the Ocean Grove oceanfront and exposed Shark River Hills slopes.
- **Flashing leaks** at chimneys, sidewalls, and skylights — the number-one leak source on Midtown and Gables two-stories with tired step flashing.
- **Valley failures** on steep Victorian rooflines where water volume and debris overwhelm old metal.
- **Low-slope porch and addition roofs** on Shark River Hills bungalows, where wind-driven rain pushes uphill under the shingles.
- **Salt-corroded fasteners** — cheap electro-galvanized nails that rust through and let shingles slide near the water.

## How we diagnose it

We don't caulk the first stain we see. We trace the leak back uphill to the real entry point, check the deck for rot around it, and inspect the surrounding field for the wear that predicts the *next* leak. On tall Ocean Grove Victorians we fly a drone so you actually see the condition of a third-story roof you'll never climb. You get photos, a plain-English explanation, and an itemized price before any work starts.
