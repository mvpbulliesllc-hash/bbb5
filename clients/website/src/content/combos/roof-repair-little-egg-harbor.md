## Leaks don't wait for the next nor'easter

Paragon Exteriors repairs roofs throughout Little Egg Harbor — wind-lifted and missing shingles, chimney and pipe-flashing leaks, blown-off ridge caps, and storm damage on the Mystic Island lagoons. Most repairs cost $350–$1,500 depending on scope, and active leaks can usually be tarped same-day or next morning because southern Ocean County is a weekly route for our crews. Call or text a photo of the damage to 848-633-6440 and we'll triage it before a truck rolls.

## What a failing roof looks like in this town

- **Shingle tabs in the yard — or the lagoon — after a blow.** Aging three-tabs with dead adhesive strips are the township's most common casualty.
- **Rust streaks running down from flashing** on bay-exposed homes: salt air off the marsh corrodes builder-grade galvanized metal from the outside in.
- **A ceiling stain near the chimney** in Parkertown's older housing, where the chimney often predates the roof by decades and the flashing predates both.
- **Lifted ridge caps and nail pops** on homes raised after Sandy — up on pilings, they catch wind they never saw at grade.
- **Gutters full of granules** on Cranberry Creek and Sunrise Bay roofs reaching the end of their first cycle.

## How we find the actual leak

Water almost never shows up where it gets in. It enters at a flashing joint or a popped nail, travels along a rafter, and drops onto your drywall ten feet away — so we don't quote off the stain. We walk the roof, check the attic side, and document everything with drone photos you keep. Then you get one itemized price. Repairs go in at coastal spec — 130 mph-rated shingles, six nails apiece — because a patch fastened to inland spec is just the next repair, scheduled early.

## When a repair is the wrong purchase

We fix roofs that deserve fixing: isolated wind damage, one failed flashing, a limb strike from the last storm. But if this is your third repair call in two years, or shingles are brittle enough that fixing one tab cracks its three neighbors, we'll say so and price the alternative honestly.
