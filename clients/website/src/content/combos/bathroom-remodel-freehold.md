## Waterproofing is the part you never see — and the part that matters most

A bathroom remodel that skips proper waterproofing is a leak waiting to happen, and it's the single biggest difference between a remodel that lasts and one that doesn't. Every tub-to-shower conversion and shower rebuild we do gets a sealed waterproofing membrane behind the tile, not just a coat of thin-set and hope. In the Victorians and early-1900s homes around downtown Freehold, this step often uncovers old subfloor damage from a slow leak nobody noticed — we deal with it as part of the job, not as a change order surprise.

## Updating a period bathroom without losing the house's character

A lot of downtown Freehold bathrooms were shoehorned into a spare room decades after the house near the Monmouth County Courthouse was built, and the plumbing shows it — cast iron drain lines, undersized supply pipes, layouts that waste half the room. We rebuild the systems to current code while keeping the footprint sensible for the house, whether that means a period-appropriate tile pattern or simply a layout that finally makes sense. Out in West Freehold and East Freehold, where most bathrooms are 1960s–2000s builder-grade, the job is usually more straightforward: fixtures, tile, and layout updates without the plumbing archaeology.
