A roof replacement in Little Silver, NJ typically runs **$9,000 to $20,000**, is completed in a **single day** on most homes, and includes the Borough permit filed by us. Every roof gets a full tear-off down to the deck — never a layover — plus a deck inspection, ice & water shield, synthetic underlayment, new flashing, and 130 mph wind-rated architectural shingles built for the Shrewsbury River exposure. Free itemized estimate: 848-633-6440.

We spec to the exposure, not the zip-code stereotype. Little Silver reads suburban on a map, but the river runs its eastern edge and Little Silver Creek, Town Neck Creek, and Parkers Creek push salt air deep into town — Seven Bridges Road is named for a reason. So corrosion-resistant edge metal, sealed rakes, and six-nail fastening are the baseline here, the same way they are out on the barrier islands.

## What a one-day tear-off looks like

- **Early-morning tear-off** to the deck, with beds and landscaping tarped first
- **Deck inspection** and rotted plywood swapped on the spot — common on station-era plank decking that has shrunk for a century
- **Ice & water shield** at eaves and valleys, **synthetic underlayment** across the whole roof
- **New drip edge and flashing** at chimneys, dormers, and walls — the layered, caulked-over flashing on the old commuter houses is where leaks start
- **130 mph architectural shingles**, six-nail coastal fastening, sealed edges
- **Ridge venting** matched to attic intake so summer heat doesn't cook the new roof from below
- **Same-day haul-away**, magnetic nail sweep, and warranty registered for you
