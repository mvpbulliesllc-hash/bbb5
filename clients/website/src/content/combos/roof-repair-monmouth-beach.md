Most roof repairs in Monmouth Beach, NJ run $450–$2,000, and in this borough the leak almost always starts at the metal — corroded chimney, valley, or wall flashing — not the shingle field, because salt off the Atlantic and the Shrewsbury River eats flashing and fasteners first. ## The symptoms we get called for

- **A stain that shows up only in a nor’easter** — wind-driven rain forced under an edge or up a wall, then gone when the sun comes out
- **Shingles lifted or missing after a blow** — common on the Ocean Avenue oceanfront and on post-Sandy elevated homes that now catch more uplift than they did at grade
- **Rust streaks or a drip near the chimney** — the salt-corroded flashing this coast is famous for
- **Granules piling in the gutters** — the shingle field aging out, which points past repair
- **Ceiling stains on interior streets off Monmouth Parkway** — often a tired pipe boot or a nail pop, an easy fix caught early

## How we diagnose it

We start on the roof, not with a guess. We trace the water to its actual entry point — flashing, valley, boot, or fastener — check the decking around it for rot, and look at the rest of the roof so we are not fixing one leak while three more brew. On the older oceanfront cottages that means checking plank decking and layered shingles; on raised Sandy rebuilds it means checking whether the fastening still matches the wind the house now takes. Every repair is documented by drone so you can see the before and after without leaving the ground.

## The honest fix-or-replace line

Here is where we will not upsell you. A young roof with one bad flashing joint gets repaired, full stop — often for a few hundred dollars. But if we are patching the third leak in two years, the shingles are curling and shedding, or the roof is past 20, a repair is money spent to delay the inevitable. We show you both numbers and let you decide, and [financing](/financing/) is there if the honest answer turns out to be a new roof.
