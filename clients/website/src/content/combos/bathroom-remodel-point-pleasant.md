## How much does a bathroom remodel cost in Point Pleasant?

Most Point Pleasant bathroom remodels land between $12,000 and $40,000. A tub-to-shower conversion or a fixture, vanity, and tile refresh sits at the low end; a full gut that relocates plumbing and reworks the layout runs toward the top. We walk your bathroom in person and hand you a free, itemized estimate before any demo starts, so there are no surprise change orders halfway through.

## Waterproofing that matches Point Pleasant's coastal setting

Nearly every home in Point Pleasant sits within a couple miles of salt water, whether that's the canal, Beaver Dam Creek, or the Manasquan riverfront out toward Bay Head Shores. That proximity means more ambient humidity and, for homes closer to the water, real flood exposure — both of which make bathroom waterproofing more than a checkbox item. We build every shower and tub surround as a fully waterproofed system: proper pan liners, moisture-resistant backer board, and sealed seams, so tile doesn't crack and subfloor doesn't rot behind the wall.

## Tub-to-shower conversions for older capes and ranches

A lot of the post-war capes, ranches, and split-levels through West Point Pleasant and Lovelandtown still have their original 1950s–70s tub-shower combo — small, dated, and not always practical as homeowners age in place. Converting to a walk-in shower typically fits the existing footprint, so plumbing rough-in stays put and the project moves faster than a full layout change.

## Do I need permits for a bathroom remodel in Point Pleasant?

Any time plumbing fixtures move or electrical work happens near a wet area, Point Pleasant Borough requires a permit and inspection. (Point Pleasant Beach is its own municipality, so we verify which building department applies before filing.) We handle the permit application and schedule every inspection ourselves, coordinating the plumber and electrician under one contract.
