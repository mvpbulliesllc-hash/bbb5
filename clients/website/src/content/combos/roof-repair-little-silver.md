### The usual suspects, by where you live

- **Station-era Victorians and foursquares** (near Markham Place and the depot): layered chimney and dormer flashing that's been caulked over for decades, plus steep slopes that shed granules where oak limbs overhang.
- **Farm-tract capes, splits, and colonials** (off Branch Avenue and Rumson Road): thin attic ventilation driving winter ice dams, and second- or third-generation shingles thinning on the weather side.
- **Point Road and Little Silver Point riverfront customs**: big river-facing planes where full northeast exposure lifts sealant strips and rusts cheap edge metal first.

## We find the entry point, not just the stain

We don't diagnose from the driveway. We get on the roof, trace the leak to where water actually enters — which is often uphill of the stain on your ceiling — and photograph it so you see exactly what we found. Salt-corroded flashing, a failed boot, a nail pop, a rotted deck edge under the drip line: the repair has to match the cause, or the stain returns with the next storm. On drone-documented jobs you get to inspect the finished fix from the air.

## When a repair is honest — and when it isn't

A targeted repair is the right move when the roof is otherwise sound, the damage is isolated, and there's real service life left. We'll fix it and stand behind it.
