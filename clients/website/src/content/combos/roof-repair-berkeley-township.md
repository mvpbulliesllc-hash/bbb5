## What breaks where in Berkeley

After enough repair calls in one town, the map writes itself:

- **Holiday City & Silver Ridge Park** — 1960s–80s ranches with roofs at or past design life: cracked pipe boots, nail pops telegraphing through shingles, and worn tabs that finally give up in a February gust.
- **Berkeley Shores & Glen Cove** — wind-lifted shingles and rain driven under ridge caps by east wind off open Barnegat Bay.
- **South Seaside Park** — nor'easter blow-offs and flashing failures, with ocean wind on one side of the island and bay wind on the other.
- **Pinewald and west of the Parkway** — pine needles damming [gutters](/gutters/) until water backs under the shingle edge, plus moss chewing up shaded north slopes.

## Finding the real leak, not the obvious one

Water travels. The stain over your kitchen table in Silver Ridge Park is often fed by a flashing gap six feet upslope, and caulking the wrong spot just buys the leak another season. Our diagnosis runs the attic and the rooftop, documents what we find in photos — drone shots included where they help — and produces an itemized quote naming each fix and its price. You approve line items, not a lump sum.

## Repair or replace: the straight answer

We repair roofs and we replace them, so we have no reason to steer you. The honest rule: isolated damage on a roof under about 15 years old is a repair, every time. But a lot of Holiday City roofs are original or riding a decades-old second layer — if shingles are curling and shedding granules across whole slopes, a $900 repair is rent, not a fix, and another payment comes due next storm. When it's close, we quote both and walk you through the 10-year math; the [repair vs. replacement guide](/blog/roof-repair-vs-replacement/) shows how we think about it. If replacement wins, most Berkeley ranches [finish in one day](/roof-replacement/berkeley-township/) and [financing](/financing/) spreads the cost.
