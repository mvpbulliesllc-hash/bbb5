## How much does a full home renovation cost in Jackson?

Whole-house renovations in Jackson generally run **$75,000–$400,000+**, set by square footage, how much is structural versus cosmetic, and the finish level. A sound ranch in Cassville or Jackson Mills getting new finishes throughout sits toward the low end; a down-to-studs renovation of a larger 1990s–2000s colonial, with moved walls and new systems, reaches the top. We scope every [full home renovation](/full-home-renovation/) in phases so the number stays transparent.

## Two very different housing stocks, one contractor

Jackson's older postwar ranches and capes around Cassville and Jackson Mills need a different renovation plan than the wave of 1990s–2000s colonials built on former farmland during the township's growth years. The ranches more often need structural and systems work brought current; the colonials more often need their builder-grade finishes and closed-off layouts reworked to match how families actually live now. Either way, one general contractor scopes it, not five separate trades bidding against each other.

## Room to expand on Jackson's big lots

Jackson's lots run larger than most of the shore towns we work in, and that room shows up in the renovations we do here — additions, opened-up floor plans, and reconfigured layouts that wouldn't fit on a tighter lot elsewhere in Ocean County. If your project is starting to look like more than a kitchen or bathroom update, a full renovation lets us plan the whole footprint at once instead of bumping into a wall we touched last year.

## How does Jackson Township handle permits for a project this size?

A whole-house renovation typically needs multiple permits — structural, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical — filed with the Jackson Township building department, each inspected at its own stage. As your general contractor, Paragon pulls every permit and manages every inspection, so you have one point of accountability for the paperwork instead of chasing it yourself across several trades.
