## The spec sheet, Barnegat edition

Every replacement gets a full tear-off (never a layover), a real deck inspection, ice & water shield at eaves and valleys, synthetic underlayment, and new drip edge and flashing throughout. Then the spec follows the geography. East of Route 9, near the municipal dock, we fasten with six nails per shingle, hand-seal starter and rake courses, and use corrosion-resistant fasteners that shrug off salt. West of the Parkway, out toward Cloverdale Farm, the pines are the threat instead: algae-resistant shingles for shaded slopes, and ventilation matched to your attic so summer heat doesn't cook the new roof from below. Weighing shingle lines? [Our Jersey Shore shingle guide](/blog/best-shingles-jersey-shore/) compares what actually survives here.

## Your install day, start to finish

Cars out of the driveway the night before. Tear-off starts around 7 AM, the deck is inspected and repaired by mid-morning, the roof is dried-in before lunch, shingles run through the afternoon, and a magnetic nail sweep of the lawn and driveway closes it out. Then we fly the drone and send you photos of the finished roof — every flashing detail, no ladder required.
