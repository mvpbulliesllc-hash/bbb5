Roof repairs in Fair Haven, NJ typically cost **$450 to $1,800**, and most fall into a handful of predictable failures: wind-lifted shingles after a river blow, cracked pipe boots, and worn flashing around the century-old chimneys and dormers on the older stock near River Road. ## Symptoms we get called for most

- **A ceiling or wall stain** that grows after a nor'easter — almost always flashing, not the field of the roof.
- **Shingles in the yard** after a northeast blow, with bare or curled tabs left on the river-facing slope.
- **Granules in the gutters** and shiny, thinning shingles on the weather side — the salt-air tell.
- **Rust streaks** below a chimney or valley, where bargain galvanized flashing gave out early.
- **Drips at the eaves in winter** — ice damming on the shaded, poorly ventilated capes off Ridge Road.

## How we diagnose it

We don't guess from the ground. We get on the roof, trace the leak to its actual entry point — which is often uphill of the stain inside — and photograph it so you see what we see. On Fair Haven's pre-war Victorians and foursquares, the usual culprit is layered chimney and dormer flashing that has been caulked over for decades; on the mid-century capes, it's thin attic ventilation driving ice dams. The fix has to match the cause, or the stain comes back next storm.

## The honest fix-or-replace call

Here's where we won't waste your money. A targeted repair is the right move when the roof is otherwise sound, the damage is isolated, and there's real service life left. We'll patch it and stand behind it.

But if the shingles are brittle and shedding across the whole river-facing slope, if you're on your third leak in two winters, or if the deck under the flashing is rotted, a patch just rents you a few months.
