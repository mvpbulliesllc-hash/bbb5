## The spec changes by street, not just by square footage

Freehold's housing went up over 120 years, and a good replacement quote reflects that:

| Your home | What we build into the spec |
|---|---|
| Borough Victorian or foursquare near Main Street | Steep-pitch staging, layered tear-off quoted upfront, plank decking priced per sheet in writing, chimney flashing rebuilt in new metal |
| East Freehold ranch, cape, or split (1960s–70s) | Ice & water shield run generously past the eave line, ridge and soffit ventilation corrected so the third roof outlasts the second |
| West Freehold colonial off Route 9 or 537 | Builder-grade shingles aging out at 18–22 years replaced with architectural shingles, attic airflow fixed so summer heat stops cooking them from below |

| Georgia or Smithburg farmhouse on a wooded lot | Limb-strike decking replaced, edge membrane under the oak canopy, gutter protection recommended where trees own the sky |

## How the one day actually goes

The crew arrives early and strips the roof to the deck — never a layover. Gapped planks and soft plywood get photographed and swapped at the per-sheet price already in your contract. Then the rebuild: ice & water shield at eaves and valleys, synthetic underlayment, new drip edge and flashing, architectural shingles, and ridge venting matched to your attic's intake. A magnetic nail sweep and same-day debris haul close it out, and we hand you drone photos of the finished roof so you can inspect it without touching a ladder.

## Two construction offices, one signature from you

"Freehold" means two building departments — Borough Hall on West Main Street or the Township's Municipal Plaza — and filing with the wrong one stalls a job. We confirm which governs your address, submit the permit, and meet the inspector. Your part is a signature.
