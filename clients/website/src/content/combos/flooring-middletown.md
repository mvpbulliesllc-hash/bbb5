## How much does new flooring cost in Middletown?

Installed flooring in Middletown typically runs **$4 to $14 per square foot**, set by material: waterproof luxury vinyl plank at the low end, engineered and solid hardwood in the middle, porcelain tile at the top once subfloor prep and setting are counted. The right choice depends as much on the room's moisture risk as it does on looks. Your free estimate breaks the cost out room by room.

## Waterproof floors for the bayshore, hardwood for the customs

Middletown's flooring needs split along the same lines as its housing stock. In Belford, Port Monmouth, Leonardo, and North Middletown, humidity off Sandy Hook Bay and the risk of occasional moisture — especially in older capes and post-Sandy elevated rebuilds — make waterproof luxury vinyl plank the practical default. In the larger custom and historic homes around Lincroft and Navesink, drier interiors and higher-end finishes make solid or engineered hardwood a better fit. In the postwar split-levels of New Monmouth and River Plaza, either works depending on the room — a kitchen or entry often gets LVP or tile, while bedrooms take hardwood.

## Can you install flooring over my existing floor?

Sometimes. Luxury vinyl and some engineered products can float over a flat, sound existing floor, which saves on demolition. But if the subfloor is uneven or water-damaged — something we look for carefully in older bayshore homes — floating new material over it just hides the problem until it resurfaces. We check the subfloor first and tell you honestly whether an overlay or a tear-out is the right call.
