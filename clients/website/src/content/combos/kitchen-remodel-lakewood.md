## How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Lakewood?

Most Lakewood kitchen remodels run **$25,000–$90,000**. A cabinet-and-counter refresh on a postwar cape sits near the low end; a full gut with a new layout, relocated plumbing, and custom cabinetry in a duplex or larger colonial reaches the top. Cabinetry, countertops, and whether walls or utilities move are what set the number. Call 848-633-6440 for a free, itemized estimate — not a ballpark.

## Lakewood's kitchens fall into two very different categories

Lakewood's building boom left thousands of 2000s–2020s duplexes and townhomes in **Westgate, Coventry Square, and Raintree** with compact, builder-grade kitchens sized for the footprint, not for the large households that often fill them. The most common request we get in these sections isn't cosmetic — it's capacity: more counter run, a bigger island or peninsula, and cabinetry reworked to fit a family that has outgrown a builder's original layout.

Older postwar capes near Downtown Lakewood and the lakes tell a different story — original 1950s–60s kitchen footprints, often disconnected from the rest of the house, that homeowners now want opened up toward the living space.

## Can a Lakewood kitchen gain capacity without expanding the footprint?

Yes — opening a kitchen to an adjoining room, moving a sink a few feet, or replacing a run of upper cabinets with taller ones that reach the ceiling can add real functional capacity to a duplex or cape kitchen without adding a single square foot to the house. As your general contractor, Paragon coordinates the carpentry, plumbing, and electrical that changes like these require, and pulls the Lakewood Township permit for any of it.

## Aging in place in Lakewood's 55+ communities

In Leisure Village and Leisure Village East, kitchen remodels tend to prioritize usability over expansion — lowered or pull-out storage, better under-cabinet lighting, lever-style faucets and hardware, and non-slip flooring underfoot. We coordinate that flooring work directly with our [flooring](/flooring/) team so the transition between kitchen and adjacent rooms is level and safe, a detail that matters more in single-story homes built for one-floor living.
