## Match the symptom to the cause

Lacey roofs fail in patterns, and after enough calls in town the patterns are predictable:

| What you're seeing | What it usually means | Where we see it most |
|---|---|---|
| Shingle tabs on the lawn after a northeast blow | Failed adhesive strips or under-nailed courses | Forked River Beach, Sunrise Beach |
| A brown ceiling ring that grows with each rain | Flashing gap at a chimney, skylight, or pipe boot | Route 9 capes and colonials |
| Gutters filling with granules | Shingles wearing out — end of life approaching | Sea Breeze at Lacey, boom-era ranches |
| Green streaks on shaded slopes | Moss feeding on trapped moisture under pine cover | Bamber Lake, west of the Parkway |

None of these improve on their own. The cheap version of every roof repair is the early one.

## We chase the source, not the stain

Water travels along rafters and sheathing before it drops, so the ring over your hallway is often fed by a flashing gap several feet upslope — and caulking the wrong spot just buys the leak another season. We start in the attic, trace the water line back to its entry point, then confirm it on the roof, photographing everything we find — drone shots included when they tell the story better. The quote that follows names each fix and its price. You approve line items, not a lump sum, and most jobs close out the same visit.

## The honesty part

We repair roofs and we replace them, so we have no dog in this fight. The rule we quote by: isolated damage on a roof under about 15 years old is a repair, full stop. But much of Lacey went up during the Oyster Creek boom, which puts the lagoon streets and the Route 9 corridor on second- or third-cycle roofs — and Sea Breeze at Lacey's builder shingles are aging out street by street. When curling and granule loss cover whole slopes, an $800 repair is rent on a failing roof, not a fix.
