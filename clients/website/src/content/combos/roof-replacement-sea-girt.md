## Built for a house that faces the open Atlantic

A roof replacement in Sea Girt typically runs **$10,000–$25,000** for architectural asphalt and finishes in a single day, with the borough permit handled for you. The spread is wide because Sea Girt roofs are: a mid-century ranch in Sea Girt Estates and a turreted 1905 cottage on the oceanfront are two different jobs. What doesn't change is the spec — every roof here gets built for direct Atlantic exposure, because there's no barrier island to take the hit first.

## The Sea Girt spec, line by line

- **Full tear-off to the deck** — no layovers hiding soft plywood, standard on the older Blvd cottages
- **Deck inspection and rot replacement** on the spot; salt air and old skylights leave more punky sheathing here than inland
- **Ice & water shield** at eaves, valleys, and every penetration — the wind-driven rain of a nor'easter climbs where felt won't hold
- **Synthetic underlayment** across the full deck
- **130 mph wind-rated architectural shingles**, six nails each, sealed starter and ridge
- **Corrosion-resistant fasteners and new flashing** at chimneys, turret transitions, and sidewalls — reusing tired flashing is where oceanfront leaks start
- **Ridge venting** matched to your intake so summer attic heat doesn't cook the shingles from below
- **Magnetic sweep and same-day haul-away** — no dumpster parked through your weekend
