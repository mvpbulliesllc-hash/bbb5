## How much does flooring installation cost in Point Pleasant?

Most flooring installation in Point Pleasant runs $4 to $14 per square foot installed, depending on the material. Waterproof LVP is usually the most cost-effective option and our most frequent recommendation for ground-level living space; tile and hardwood run higher depending on pattern complexity and how much subfloor prep is needed. We measure the space in person and hand you a free, itemized estimate before any material is ordered.

## Waterproof flooring for a near-water town

Nearly every home in Point Pleasant sits within a couple miles of salt water, and neighborhoods along Beaver Dam Creek, the Point Pleasant Canal, and Bay Head Shores face more direct moisture exposure than homes further inland in West Point Pleasant or Lovelandtown. For ground-level rooms in those areas, we lean toward waterproof luxury vinyl plank — it holds up to humidity, minor leaks, and occasional tidal flooding without the swelling or delamination that traditional laminate or solid hardwood is prone to. It also handles daily wear well in homes that see sandy feet and boat gear tracked in from the water.

## Tile and hardwood where they make sense

Not every room calls for LVP. Bathrooms and entryways in capes and ranches through the central borough often do best with tile for its water resistance underfoot, and dry, upper-level living spaces are still good candidates for engineered or solid hardwood where moisture isn't a daily concern. We walk through material tradeoffs room by room rather than defaulting to one product for the whole house.

## Matching flooring across an open layout

If you've opened up a wall between the kitchen and living room, or you're planning to, flooring is often the detail that makes the new layout look finished rather than patched together. We plan transitions and material direction so the floor reads as one continuous surface, whether the underlying rooms started on different subfloor heights or different original materials.
