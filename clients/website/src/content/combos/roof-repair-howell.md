## What's failing depends on when your street was built

Howell went up in waves, and each wave breaks in its own way:

| If you live in… | What usually fails | What the fix looks like |
|---|---|---|
| A Candlewood or Ramtown split or ranch (late 60s–70s) | Brittle shingles on a second or third roof, tired chimney and step flashing, nail pops | Flashing rebuilt in new metal, targeted shingle runs — plus an honest read on how long the rest holds |
| An 80s–90s colonial off Route 9 or toward Adelphia | Builder-grade tabs losing their seal strips, valleys wearing thin across multi-gable rooflines | Lifted runs re-fastened and sealed; valley rebuilds where the mat is gone |
| A wooded lot in Southard, Ardena, or West Farms, or near the reservoir | Limb punctures, moss along the shade line, ice dams at eaves the winter sun never reaches | Puncture and decking repairs, eave membrane, ventilation corrections that stop the melt-freeze cycle |
| A newer or 55+ community home (2000s) | Cracked pipe boots, ridge vent leaks | Quick, small-dollar fixes — usually the cheapest row on this table |

## The stain is the exit, not the entry

A ceiling stain marks where water gave up, not where it got in — the actual breach is often several rafters upslope. We trace the path from the attic first, confirm it on the roof, and document everything with drone photos, so the itemized quote covers the real problem instead of the visible symptom. Texting us photos of the stain when you call speeds up the whole visit.
