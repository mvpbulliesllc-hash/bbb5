## What that stain is telling you

Manchester's mix of 1960s–80s village ranches and deep Pine Barrens lots produces predictable failures. Match your symptom:

| What you're seeing | What it usually means here |
|---|---|
| Ceiling stain that appears after a February thaw | Ice dam on a long, shaded ranch eave forcing meltwater under the shingles |
| Brown ring at the chimney after wind-driven rain | Original 1960s–70s flashing at the end of its life |
| Damp corner in the Florida room or enclosed porch | Failed seams on the low-slope addition — not the main roof at all |
| Black streaks and green fuzz on a pine-shaded slope | Algae and moss lifting shingle edges and shortening their life |
| Grit in the gutters, tabs on the lawn after storms | Shingles aging out — common on third-cycle village roofs |
| Fresh dent or hole after a storm | Dead pine limb strike; the damage under the shingle is often wider than the hole |
