## The spec changes east of Route 9

A northeast blow crosses miles of open Barnegat Bay before it reaches a lagoon roofline, so on Forked River Beach and Sunrise Beach we install 130 mph wind-rated architectural shingles with six nails per shingle, hand-sealed starter and rake courses, and corrosion-resistant fasteners that shrug off salt air. West of the Parkway the threat flips: standard architectural shingles hold fine toward Bamber Lake, but we pay extra attention to ridge ventilation and clean drip-edge detail, because pine litter and trapped moisture kill those roofs long before wind does.

## Permit, tear-off, dinner

We file the re-roof permit with the Lacey Township Building Department on West Lacey Road before a shingle moves. Install day starts around 7 AM with full tear-off — never a layover — and a real deck inspection. Houses from the Oyster Creek boom of the '60s and '70s often hide soft plywood under old layers; we swap bad sheets on the spot at the per-sheet price already printed in your quote. Then ice & water shield at eaves and valleys, synthetic underlayment, new drip edge and flashing, shingles to the spec above, ridge venting matched to your attic — and a magnetic nail sweep before we leave. You get drone photos of the finished roof, not a "trust us" from the driveway.
