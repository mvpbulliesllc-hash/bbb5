## Waterproofing is the part you can't see — and the part that matters most

A bathroom remodel lives or dies on what's behind the tile. In Howell's older housing stock — particularly the late-60s and 70s splits and ranches through Candlewood and Ramtown — original mortar-bed showers and cast-iron tubs often sit on subfloor that's been absorbing slow leaks for years without anyone noticing. We open the wall and check the subfloor before finalizing scope, install a proper waterproof membrane system (not just a coat of paint-on sealant), and slope every shower pan correctly to the drain. That's the work nobody sees in the finished photo — and the reason a remodel either lasts twenty years or fails again in three.

## Can you convert my tub to a walk-in shower?

Yes — this is one of the most common requests we get across Howell's 55+ communities and from longtime homeowners aging in place in their Candlewood or Ramtown ranch. We handle full tub-to-shower conversions with a properly framed and waterproofed pan, glass or tile surround, and fixtures sized for the new layout. That often means a curbless or low-curb entry, in-wall blocking for grab bars — installed now or ready for later — and slip-resistant flooring, done right the first time instead of retrofitted after a fall.

## What changes by neighborhood and housing stock

- **Late-60s/70s splits and ranches (Candlewood, Ramtown)** — Original tile, cast-iron tubs, and dated single-vanity layouts. Work often includes full gut-and-waterproof, updated ventilation, and layout tweaks within the existing footprint.
- **1980s–90s colonials off Route 9** — Builder-grade fiberglass tub/shower units and laminate vanities. Remodels here commonly upgrade to tile showers, double vanities, and better lighting.
- **55+ communities** — Focus tends toward accessibility: curbless showers, comfort-height fixtures, and slip-resistant surfaces.
