## The leak you can stop today, and the one you can't

Most Sea Girt roof leaks trace to three things: shingles lifted or torn by nor'easter wind, flashing that's failed at a chimney or turret transition, or a pipe boot cracked by sun and salt. A targeted repair runs **$350–$1,800** and often happens the same visit; an active leak gets a tarp same or next day. The honest part comes when we're up there — a repair only makes sense if the rest of the roof has real life left, and at the oceanfront that's not a given.

## Symptoms we get called for in Sea Girt

- **Ridge caps lifted or gone** after a blow — the first thing to let go on ocean-facing slopes
- **Ceiling or wall stains** appearing only during driving rain, dry the rest of the year
- **Rust streaks or granule piles** in the gutters, a sign the field shingles are aging out
- **Leaks at the chimney, skylight, or turret** — almost always flashing, not the shingles
- **Interior drips near an exterior wall** where wind-driven water climbed behind the step flashing

## How we diagnose it

We don't sealant-smear the stain. Water travels along rafters and underlayment before it drops, so we track it back to the actual breach — then confirm with a drone pass so you see the damage from above, not just take our word. Salt-corroded fasteners, a nail that's backed out, a boot split at the pipe: on a coastal roof the cause is often small and specific, and finding it beats replacing what isn't broken.
