## How much does a bathroom remodel cost in Middletown?

Most Middletown bathroom remodels run $12,000 to $40,000, depending on scope. A hall-bath refresh with a tub-to-shower conversion sits at the low end; a primary suite with a custom tiled shower, double vanity, and relocated plumbing runs toward the top — common in the larger homes around Lincroft and Navesink. Every estimate is itemized by scope before any demo starts, so you know the number going in.

## Waterproofing matters more on the bayshore

A bathroom is the wettest room in any house, and the failures you can't see — bad waterproofing behind the tile — are the ones that rot framing from the inside. That risk runs higher in Belford, Port Monmouth, and Leonardo, where bay-adjacent humidity keeps the air damp most of the year and post-Sandy elevated homes sometimes have different framing to waterproof around. We build every shower with a proper membrane or backer system, a sloped pan, and sealed corners before a single tile goes up, whether the home sits three blocks from Sandy Hook Bay or up in Lincroft.

## What a Paragon bathroom remodel covers

## Do bathroom remodels need permits in Middletown Township?

Most bathroom remodels move or add plumbing and electrical, and Middletown Township requires a permit and inspection whenever that happens. We pull it through the township's construction office and schedule the inspection ourselves. A straight fixture swap with no relocated utilities sometimes skips the permit step — we'll tell you honestly which category your project falls into before we ever start demo.
