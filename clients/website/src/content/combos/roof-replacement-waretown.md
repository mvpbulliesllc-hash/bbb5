## One town, two spec sheets

Route 9 splits Waretown's weather in half: open Barnegat Bay to the east, pitch pines out toward Wells Mills to the west. Every replacement gets the same foundation — full tear-off, deck inspection, ice & water shield at eaves and valleys, synthetic underlayment, new drip edge and flashing — then the spec follows your address:

| | Bayside — Skippers Cove, Sands Point Harbor | Pines side — Brookville, off Route 532 |
|---|---|---|
| Shingle | 130 mph wind-rated architectural | Algae-resistant architectural |
| Fastening | Six nails per shingle, hand-sealed starter and rake courses | Six-nail pattern, standard sealing |
| Metal | Corrosion-resistant flashing and fasteners against salt air | Standard flashing, gutters planned for needle loads |
| Enemy | Open-water gusts and wind-driven rain | Shade, moss, and attic heat under tree cover |

The Route 9 village capes near Waretown Lake are the wildcard — the oldest roofs in town, often layered, sometimes soft underneath. Bad plywood gets swapped on the spot at the per-sheet price already printed in your quote, so tear-off day never turns into a renegotiation.
