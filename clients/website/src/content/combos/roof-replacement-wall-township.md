## Built to the spec your side of Wall actually needs

Wall covers 31 square miles, and the right roof changes as you drive across it:

- **West Belmar and Manasquan Park** sit barely two miles from the ocean, with the Shark and Manasquan rivers channeling nor'easter wind straight across the east end. These postwar capes and ranches get 130 mph wind-rated shingles, six-nail fastening, and sealed edges — no exceptions.
- **Glendola and New Bedford** split-levels from the 1960s–80s usually have undersized soffit and ridge ventilation cooking shingles from the attic side. We correct the airflow during the re-roof, because a new roof over a starved attic dies young.
- **The wooded west toward Allaire and Allenwood** — 1990s–2000s colonials with big, cut-up rooflines. Valleys and dormers punish lazy flashing, and heavy oak shade breeds algae, so these get algae-resistant shingles and new flashing at every transition.

## How replacement day runs

Tear-off starts early and goes down to bare deck. We inspect the plywood and swap anything soft on the spot, then build back up: ice & water shield at eaves and valleys, synthetic underlayment, new drip edge and flashing, shingles fastened to the high-wind spec, ridge venting matched to your attic's intake. A magnetic nail sweep and same-day debris haul-away close it out — plus **drone photos of the finished roof**, which matters on a treed Allaire lot where you'll never see half of it from the ground. It's the same process we ran through our 30-roofs-in-30-days push in May 2026: the schedule flexes, the spec doesn't.
