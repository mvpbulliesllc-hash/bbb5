## Read the symptom before you spend

Most roof repairs in Tinton Falls cost **$350–$1,800**, and the smart move is diagnosing the cause before paying to chase the stain. In a wooded borough, the same ceiling spot can come from four very different failures — a limb puncture, worn flashing, an ice dam, or lifted shingles — and each has a different fix and a different price. We find the actual source, not just the drip.

## What we see most on Tinton Falls roofs

- **Limb and branch punctures.** Oaks along Sycamore Avenue and the Shafto Road corridor drop deadwood in every nor'easter. A single puncture can open the deck and soak insulation — we tarp it fast, then rebuild the sheathing and re-shingle the section.
- **Ice-dam leaks.** Gutters packed with November oak litter back January meltwater under the shingle edge. The stain shows in a bedroom ceiling while the roof looks fine from the driveway. The real cure pairs a repair with [gutter protection](/gutters/) and better eave detail.
- **Flashing failures.** Shaded, damp chimney and wall flashing corrodes and pulls loose — the leading leak on the older north-end splits and colonials near the Eatontown line. Often a reseal or re-flash, not a new roof.
- **Wind-lifted and moss-lifted shingles.** Coastal gusts still reach inland Tinton Falls, and moss on north slopes creeps under tabs and breaks the seal.
