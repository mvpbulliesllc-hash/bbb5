Most bathroom remodels in Jackson run **$12,000–$40,000**. A hall-bath refresh with a tub-to-shower swap sits toward the low end; a primary suite with a custom tiled shower, double vanity, and relocated plumbing reaches the top. Tile, waterproofing, and moving fixtures are the real cost drivers on every [bathroom remodel](/bathroom-remodel/) we quote, and your estimate itemizes each one so you can see exactly where the money goes.

## Aging-in-place bathrooms for Four Seasons and Westlake

A large share of the bathroom work we do in Jackson is in the township's two big 55+ communities. Original tub-and-tile bathrooms in Four Seasons at Metedeconk Lakes and Westlake are reaching the age where homeowners start planning ahead — replacing a tub with a curbless or low-threshold shower, adding grab-bar blocking behind the tile, and improving lighting, all without the room looking institutional. We coordinate any HOA approval those communities call for.

## Waterproofing behind the tile is what actually matters

Every bathroom fails from the inside if the waterproofing is wrong, whether the house is a postwar cape in Cassville or a 2005 colonial off County Line Road. We build showers with a proper waterproof membrane or backer system, a correctly sloped pan, and sealed penetrations before tile goes up — the part nobody sees is the part that decides whether the room lasts.

## Do I need a permit for a bathroom remodel in Jackson Township?

Most full remodels do. Moving or adding plumbing and electrical — which tub-to-shower conversions and full gut jobs almost always involve — requires a permit and inspection from the Jackson Township building department. We prepare and file that paperwork and schedule the inspections as your general contractor, so the process doesn't land on you.
