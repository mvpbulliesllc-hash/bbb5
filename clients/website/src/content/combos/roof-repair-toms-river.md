## Fast, honest roof repair across Toms River

A roof leak in Toms River rarely announces itself politely — it shows up as a ceiling stain after a bay-driven downpour or a pile of shingle tabs on the lawn after a nor'easter. We find the actual source (which is often several feet uphill from where the water shows inside), fix it right, and tell you the truth about whether the roof has years left or is telling you it's time.

## What we repair most in Toms River

- **Wind-lifted and missing shingles** — common east of Hooper Avenue and out in Ortley Beach, re-secured to coastal spec so they hold next time
- **Chimney and wall flashing leaks** — the number-one true source of "roof" leaks on Toms River's 1970s–80s housing stock
- **Pipe boots and vent seals** — cracked rubber boots are a cheap fix that saves an expensive ceiling
- **Valley and nail-pop leaks** — from decades of freeze-thaw on older Holiday City and North Dover roofs
- **Emergency tarping** — to stop an active leak today while we schedule the permanent repair

## The diagnosis comes first

We don't guess. We photograph the roof (drone where it helps), trace the water path, and show you what's actually failing before we quote. If the honest answer is that a $1,200 repair only buys a year on a worn-out roof, we'll say so and walk you through a [full replacement](/roof-replacement/toms-river/) and [financing](/financing/) instead — no pressure either way.
