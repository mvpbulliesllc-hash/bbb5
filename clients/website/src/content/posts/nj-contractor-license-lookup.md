## The 2-minute check that filters out most bad roofers

Every home improvement contractor operating in New Jersey — roofers included — must register with the
**NJ Division of Consumer Affairs** and display their **HIC registration number** on contracts,
invoices, and advertising. Verifying one takes about two minutes and instantly filters out most of the
bad actors: storm chasers, out-of-state crews, and "licensed & insured" websites that never publish a
number.

## Step-by-step lookup

1. **Open the state registry.** Go to njconsumeraffairs.gov and click **"Verify a License."**
2. **Pick the profession.** Choose **Home Improvement Contractor** from the list.
3. **Search.** Enter the business name or, better, the 13VH registration number from their contract or website.
4. **Read the record.** Confirm three things: the **legal business name matches** who you're dealing with, the registration is **active (not expired, suspended or revoked)**, and the address on file makes sense for a local company.
5. **Cross-check insurance.** Ask for the certificate of insurance and call the agent listed on it — a COI is only as good as its expiration date.

## Red flags the lookup catches

- **No number anywhere** — website, truck, or proposal just says "Licensed & Insured."
- **A borrowed number** — the registry name doesn't match the company on your contract.
- **Recently expired or brand-new registration** right after a big storm (classic storm-chaser pattern).
- **Refusal to pull permits** — unregistered contractors can't, so they'll pressure you to skip them.
