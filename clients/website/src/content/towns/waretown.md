## Bay in front, Pinelands behind — roofing Waretown right

Paragon Exteriors is a licensed, fully insured roofing and exteriors contractor (NJ HIC #13VH13814500) serving Waretown and the rest of Ocean Township, NJ. We replace and repair roofs, install siding, windows, doors, and gutters, and finish most roof replacements in a single day — from Skippers Cove lagoon homes to Greenbriar Oceanaire ranches. Estimates are free and itemized: call 848-633-6440.

Waretown fights weather on two fronts, which almost no other Shore town does. East of Route 9, homes stare across open Barnegat Bay and take salt air, wind-driven rain, and nor'easter gusts with nothing to slow them down — Long Beach Island is too far across the water to shelter anything. West of the Parkway, pitch pines shade roofs, streak shingles with algae, pack gutters with needles, and drop limbs in every storm. The right roof spec is different on each side of town, and we quote it that way.

## What each part of Waretown actually needs

**Skippers Cove and Sands Point Harbor.** The lagoon sections date largely to the 1960s and 70s, and plenty of houses that started as summer getaways now work year-round. These roofs face the bay head-on, so we install 130 mph wind-rated architectural shingles with six-nail fastening, sealed rake and eave edges, and flashing chosen to shrug off salt — the same spec we detail in our [coastal nor'easter protection guide](/blog/coastal-roofing-noreaster-protection/).

**Greenbriar Oceanaire.** Roughly 1,400 homes around the golf course, most built in the 2000s — which puts the original builder-grade shingles at or past 20 years right now. If your street has already sprouted a few new roofs, yours is on the same clock. More on this below.

**The Route 9 village.** The older capes and ranches near Waretown Lake carry some of the town's oldest roofs. Layered shingles and soft deck plywood are common here; we price tear-offs honestly and swap bad decking on the spot instead of shingling over it.

**Brookville and the Wells Mills side.** On the wooded lots off Route 532 — out past Wells Mills County Park and Albert Music Hall — the enemies are shade, moss, and needle-choked gutters rather than wind. Algae-resistant shingles and [gutter protection](/gutters/) earn their keep out here.
