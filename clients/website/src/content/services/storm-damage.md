## Storm damage repair, from claim to finished roof

If a nor’easter, windstorm, or fallen tree just damaged your roof, the right order of operations is: stop the water now with an emergency tarp, document the damage before anything gets covered or cleaned up, then file the insurance claim with a licensed local contractor at your side to meet the adjuster. At the Jersey Shore most sudden, weather-caused roof damage is covered by your homeowners policy minus the deductible — but only if it's documented as storm damage, not written off as old-roof wear. Paragon Exteriors handles all of it, and we never ask you to sign your claim away to do it.

## First 48 hours: stop the damage, save the evidence

Water moving through a roof doesn't wait for a claim number. The moment interior leaking starts, drywall, insulation, and framing are on the clock. Our storm response is built around that reality:

- **Emergency tarping** to stop active water intrusion — usually reimbursable as loss mitigation
- **Full drone documentation** before we tarp: high-resolution overhead and close-up imagery of every lifted shingle, cracked flashing, and impact point
- **Interior damage photos** of ceilings, walls, and attic so nothing gets missed in the scope
- **A written, dated damage report** you can hand to your carrier the same day
- **We meet the adjuster on-site** so the inspection is thorough and the estimate reflects the real damage

Drones matter more than people expect. An adjuster standing in your driveway can't see the back slope or the ridge; our aerial imagery puts the damage on the record from angles a ground inspection misses — and that's often the difference between an approved claim and a lowball.

## Never sign an AOB with a storm-chaser

After every big Shore storm, out-of-town crews flood the neighborhoods knocking on doors, pushing an **Assignment of Benefits (AOB)** form. Signing it hands your insurance claim and your roof to a company you'll never find again.

## Built for Jersey Shore storms

Ocean and Monmouth County roofs live in the path of coastal weather that inland roofs never face — nor’easters that stall offshore and pound the coast for days, sustained wind off the bay and ocean, and horizontal rain that exploits every weak seam. Barrier-island, bayfront, and back-bay homes take the hardest hits, and damage is often invisible from the ground: a strip of shingles lifted on the ocean-facing slope, flashing peeled at a chimney, a ridge cap gone. After any major blow, it's worth a free inspection even if the ceiling isn't leaking yet — because by the time it is, the deck below is already soaked. See our guide to [protecting your roof from nor'easters](/blog/coastal-roofing-noreaster-protection/) and [how NJ wind and storm insurance claims work](/blog/roof-insurance-claim-nj/).

## When repair becomes replacement

Sometimes a storm is the final straw on a roof that was already near the end. If the damage is widespread, or your roof is 20+ years old and losing the fight, a [full roof replacement](/roof-replacement/) is often the smarter claim — and your policy may owe most of it. When the damage is isolated, a targeted [roof repair](/roof-repair/) puts you back to weather-tight for far less. We tell you honestly which one your roof needs, and we do both.
