## How much does a bathroom remodel cost in New Jersey?

Bathroom remodels in Ocean and Monmouth County typically run **$12,000–$40,000** — a hall-bath refresh at the low end, a primary suite with a custom tiled shower, double vanity, and relocated plumbing at the top. Tile, waterproofing, and moving fixtures set the number. You get an itemized estimate by scope, so you can see where the money actually goes.

## The part nobody sees decides everything

A bathroom is the wettest room in the house, and the failures are the ones you can't see: bad waterproofing behind the tile that rots framing from the inside. We build showers with a **proper waterproof membrane, a correctly sloped pan, and sealed penetrations** before a single tile goes up. On coastal homes where humidity never really drops, that discipline matters even more.

## What a Paragon bathroom remodel covers

- **Demolition** down to studs where the scope calls for it, dust-controlled
- **Licensed plumbing and electrical**, including tub-to-shower conversions, with permits pulled by us
- **Waterproofing** — membrane or backer system, sloped pan, sealed corners and penetrations
- **Tile** — shower walls, floors, and niches, set and grouted clean
- **Vanity, top, and fixtures**, plus mirrors, lighting, and exhaust ventilation sized to the room
- **Heated floors and accessibility features** where you want them
- **Final walkthrough** before we call it complete
