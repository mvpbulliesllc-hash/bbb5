## What goes on before a single panel does

Most siding failures we're called to fix weren't panel failures — they were installation shortcuts hiding underneath. Every Paragon siding job includes:

- **Complete strip-off** of the existing siding — we don't bury old problems under new panels
- **Sheathing inspection** with rotted or delaminated sections replaced on the spot
- **Premium house wrap**, seams taped, laps shingled the right direction
- **Flashing at every window, door, and penetration** — the detail that separates dry walls from hidden rot
- **Optional rigid foam or insulated panels** for continuous insulation over the studs
- **Corrosion-resistant fasteners** on homes near saltwater, nailed to spec — vinyl pinned too tight buckles in the first July heat wave
- **Matching trim, corners, and soffit work** so the finished house reads as one design, not a patch job

## The salt-air problem, solved at the spec level

Jersey Shore siding fights on two fronts. Sun and salt attack the surface — that's the chalky film on your palm when you touch a 15-year-old wall. Wind attacks the fastening — a nor'easter gust that gets under one unlocked course can unzip a whole elevation. We counter both: fade-resistant capstock or factory-finished fiber cement for the surface, heavier panel gauge and fully locked, wind-rated installation for the structure. It's the same philosophy we bring to every roof we install: build for the weather your house actually gets, not the national average.
