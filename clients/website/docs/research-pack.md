# Build-Brief Research Pack: Paragon Exteriors NJ / One Day Home Improvements — NJ Home Improvement & Roofing (Ocean & Monmouth County)

## TL;DR
- **Win with hyper-local, entity-consistent fundamentals, not AI gimmicks.** The single highest-ROI stack for both sites is: unique per-town service pages + fully optimized Google Business Profiles + steady review velocity + a linked JSON-LD @graph. Google confirmed (June 15, 2026) that llms.txt does nothing for its Search/AI Overviews, and AI Overview citations are earned by the same signals that win the local map pack — so build one hardened foundation that serves classic SEO, AI Overviews, and answer engines simultaneously.
- **The NJ market is storm-driven and coastal — that is the wedge.** Post-nor'easter/hail demand surges, coastal wind + salt-air roof failure, high wind-deductible insurance claims, and "storm chaser" distrust are the exact intent moments the national players (Power Home Remodeling, DaBella) do NOT serve well. Paragon should own storm-response, insurance-claim, and shore-home content per town.
- **Every ad and page must carry HIC #13VH13814500, and every "free"/bundle/financing offer must be disclosed clean-and-conspicuous.** NJ's Consumer Fraud Act makes even a technical omission a per se violation with treble damages + attorney's fees. The registration number is legally required in all advertising; the FTC "Free" Guide and TILA/Reg Z govern "free gutters with a roof" and "0% financing" claims.

## Key Findings

### 1. Keyword research (NJ / Ocean & Monmouth)
- Roofing is the most expensive home-services vertical to buy: per **LocaliQ's 2025 Home Services Search Ads Benchmarks (localiq.com; 3,211 US campaigns)**, Roofing & Gutters posted the highest home-services CPL at **$228.15** and among the highest CPCs at **$10.70**; category-wide averages were **$90.92 CPL and $7.85 CPC**. This is why organic/GBP is the cheaper long-term channel — exactly the paragonexteriorsnj.com play. (Note: an earlier LocaliQ vintage cited ~$186.79 roofing CPL; the current figure is $228.15.)
- Search intent splits three ways per service line, and each deserves its own page: **emergency/transactional** ("emergency roof repair near me," "roof leak repair"), which surges after storms; **commercial-investigation** ("roof replacement Toms River NJ," "best roofers Ocean County"); and **informational** ("how much does a roof replacement cost in NJ," "roof repair vs replacement," "do I need a permit for a deck in NJ").
- Local phrasing patterns that convert: `[service] + [town] + NJ` ("roof replacement Brick NJ"), `[service] near me`, `[trade] Ocean County`, `storm damage roofing [town]`, and question queries. National keyword-volume estimates (directional; tools disagree widely): "roof replacement" ~49,500/mo, "roofing contractor near me" ~90,000/mo, "roof repair" quoted anywhere from ~49,500 to ~135,000/mo.
- Northeast/Jersey-specific language to weave into copy: **nor'easter, coastal/wind-driven rain, salt-air corrosion, shore homes, barrier island (LBI), wind deductible, ACV vs RCV, ice dams, GAF Timberline HDZ, Owens Corning, ice-and-water shield.**
- Per-service anchors and NJ cost ranges to publish: Roofing (repair/replacement/storm/commercial/inspection; full replacement ~$12k–$20k, storm repairs $300–$15k+), Siding (James Hardie fiber cement, vinyl), Windows, Doors, Gutters/leaders/guards, Decks, Exterior paint, HVAC (AC-install searches peak Apr–Jun; heating/furnace Sep–Oct), Solar, Flooring (LVT/hardwood), Kitchen remodel (~$20k–$150k), Bathroom remodel (~$15k–$75k), Man cave/game room (basement build-out ~$60k–$120k+), Full home renovation (~$150k–$500k+). Cost pages with honest NJ price ranges are among the highest-intent, most AI-citable assets you can build.

### 2. Schema / structured data (2026)
- Use a single site-wide `<script type="application/ld+json">` with an `@graph`, plus one page-level graph per page. Do NOT scatter four separate script blocks.
- Type strategy: primary business node `["LocalBusiness","RoofingContractor"]` on paragonexteriorsnj.com (RoofingContractor is a live schema.org type as of V30, 2026); for the full-scope expansion use `HomeAndConstructionBusiness`/`GeneralContractor`, and add `HVACBusiness` where relevant. Each service page gets its own `Service` node (serviceType + provider @id + areaServed). Location pages get `LocalBusiness` + `BreadcrumbList`. FAQ sections get `FAQPage` only when the Q&A is visible on-page.
- @id linking pattern: give every node a stable @id fragment on the canonical URL (`#business`, `#service`, `#webpage`, `#breadcrumb`). Site-wide `Organization`/`WebSite` defined once; page nodes reference them by @id.
- Required/rewarded fields: PostalAddress (all components), telephone in +1 format (+1-848-633-6440), geo coordinates, `areaServed` listing all real towns, `sameAs` to GBP/Yelp/BBB/Facebook, `aggregateRating` + `Review` (genuine only), `openingHoursSpecification`, `priceRange`, `paymentAccepted` incl. Financing.
- NAP must match character-for-character across schema, GBP, and every citation. "Ste" vs "Suite" fragments the entity and suppresses the map pack.
- 2026 reality check on schema's AI value: Google says no special schema is required for AI features, and the most rigorous study to date (**Ahrefs, May 11 2026, 1,885 treated vs ~4,000 control pages**) found schema's effect on AI citations statistically indistinguishable from zero (AI Mode +2.4%, ChatGPT +2.2%, AI Overviews −4.6%). Widely-repeated agency claims of "3x more AI answers with schema" (BrightEdge) have no traceable primary source — do not brief schema as an AI-citation lever. Schema still earns classic rich results, powers local-pack accuracy, and disambiguates the entity, which is reason enough to implement it well.

### 3. GEO / AEO / LLM search (2026)
- **llms.txt verdict: ship a lightweight one, expect nothing from Google.** Adoption ~5-10% of top sites; SE Ranking's ~300k-domain study found no statistically significant correlation with AI citations; Google's June 15, 2026 AI-optimization guide states it has no effect on Search or AI Overviews; Perplexity and Claude do fetch it and coding agents depend on it. Treat it as cheap forward-compatibility (especially fitting for the AI-visualizer sister site), not an SEO lever.
- **What actually moves AI citations:** (1) accurate business-type declaration in schema; (2) verifiable NAP consistency across the web; (3) recent reviews + quality citations; (4) content that directly answers local questions with FAQ sections. AI Overviews are Gemini-powered and pull from the same Knowledge Graph/trust signals as the local pack — there is no separate algorithm to game.
- **Answer-block formatting:** lead every section with a self-contained 40-80 word direct answer under a question-style H2/H3 that mirrors how people prompt ("How much does a roof replacement cost in Toms River?"). Use semantic HTML5, clean H1→H2→H3 hierarchy, and tables/bullet lists for data (tables get cited more than prose). Publish pricing logic — hiding cost removes you from cost-answer citations.
- Off-site entity signals matter more than any single on-page factor: consistent brand mentions, Reddit/Nextdoor participation, and video. **YouTube is the #1-cited domain in Google AI Overviews at ~29.5% citation share (BrightEdge, 30M sources, May 2024–Sep 2025), cited far more than any other video platform, with its share up ~34% in six months (Ahrefs Brand Radar)** — this ties your video program directly to AI visibility.
- Freshness: pages updated within ~60-90 days are meaningfully more likely to be cited; refresh service/cost pages with current pricing and recent project examples.

### 4. Local SEO (2026)
- Map-pack ranking weight, per **Whitespark's 2026 Local Search Ranking Factors survey (47 experts, 187 factors, published Nov 6 2025 by Darren Shaw)**: **GBP ~32%, reviews ~20% (up from 16% in 2023), on-page ~19%, links ~15%, behavioral ~8%, citations ~7%.** GBP alone carries about a third; reviews have risen in weight.
- GBP: exact-match name (no keyword stuffing — that's a 2026 penalty flag), precise primary category ("Roofing Contractor") + accurate secondaries, every service with a ~300-char description, 20+ real photos (Google Vision AI now reads photos to verify expertise), weekly posts, seeded Q&A, respond to every review. Profiles silent 30+ days measurably lose visibility.
- Reviews: velocity beats volume — steady recent reviews containing service/town keywords outperform a big stale pile. Corroborating primary data: **GBPs crossing 100 reviews saw ~31% YoY lead-volume growth, and profiles with 50+ reviews win ~4.4x more clicks than those with under 5 (Visionary Marketing GBP crawl, 2026, n≈180,000).** Never buy reviews (AI pattern-detection sweeps de-rank/suspend).
- **Service-area pages without doorway penalties:** one genuinely unique page per town — town in title/H1, plain-language local description, real local proof (jobs completed in that town, customer quotes, neighborhood/landmark references), embedded map, NAP, town-specific FAQs, visible CTA. Never template-swap the city name across 38 near-identical pages — that triggers doorway/duplicate demotion. Fewer, deeper pages beat many thin ones. With 38 towns, build in waves: fully bespoke content for highest-population/highest-intent towns first (Toms River, Brick, Lakewood, Jackson, Howell, Middletown), expanding as real project photos/testimonials accumulate.
- Topic-cluster internal linking: pillar (e.g., "Roofing") → service pages (repair/replacement/storm) → town pages → supporting blog posts, with descriptive anchor text.
- Core Web Vitals: LCP <2.5s, INP <200ms, CLS <0.1; mobile-first (most roofing searches are mobile, often from the driveway). Sub-2.5s load is effectively required.
- Emerging risk: Google's AI Local Packs (mobile US, appearing on a rising ~8% of local keywords) show fewer businesses and strip the click-to-call button — and feature far fewer unique businesses (**~5,943 unique businesses in AI local packs vs ~18,330 in regular 3-packs, per Joy Hawkins/Sterling Sky, "The State of Local SEO in 2026"**). Another reason to diversify to organic + AI citation + direct brand demand.

Most are roofing/exterior-focused; GAF Timberline HDZ and Owens Corning Platinum are the common trust badges.
- **Where a nimble local operator wins:** (a) emergency/repair and small jobs the nationals decline; (b) storm/insurance-claim expertise content per town; (c) full-scope home improvement (kitchen/bath/HVAC/solar/flooring/man cave) under one licensed roof — most local roofers do NOT cover interior remodels, so Paragon's breadth is a genuine content and positioning gap to exploit; (d) the AI-photo-visualizer on onedayhomeimprovements.com as a conversion differentiator; (e) manufacturer certification + honest, no-storm-chaser positioning; (f) named-expert E-E-A-T (owner bios, real crew) that both nationals and thin local sites lack.

### 6. Bundle / offer GTM & compliance
- **What's used successfully in home improvement:** "free gutters with a roof or siding project," "buy 3 windows get 1 free," bundle-and-save multi-trade discounts (roof + siding + gutters), seasonal % off, and promotional financing ("0% for 12/18 months," "$0 down"). DaBella and Window World are the reference models.
- **FTC "Free" Guide (16 CFR 251.1):** all terms/conditions of a "free" offer must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously *at the outset*, in close conjunction with the word "Free" — not in an asterisked footnote. The paired item must be sold at its regular price (the bona fide price for the prior 30 days), not marked up to cover the "free" item. A single "free" offer should not run more than 6 months in any 12-month period in a trade area (≤3 offers/12 months). "Gift"/"bonus" substitutes don't dodge the rule.
- **TILA/Reg Z (12 CFR 1026.16):** if you state "no interest," you must also state "if paid in full" plus the deferred-interest period, clearly and conspicuously. If you advertise a rate/payment or trigger terms, the required disclosures follow. Standard contractor practice: financing provided by unaffiliated third-party lenders, "on approved credit," "not all buyers qualify," "terms are estimates," and state the contractor is neither broker nor lender.
- **NJ overlay:** the HIC registration number must appear in all advertisements (statutory — N.J.S.A. 56:8-144 requires registrants to "prominently display their registration numbers... in all advertisements distributed within this State"); "waiving the insurance deductible" is illegal in NJ and a scam flag — do NOT offer it; every home-improvement contract over $500 must be written with mandated terms and a 3-day right of rescission.

### 7. Viral short-form video hooks (2026)
- **The mechanic:** completion rate drives distribution; the first ~1.5-3 seconds decide it. Target ~70% retention past 3s. Lead with the damage shot or finished install — never a logo intro. Reels/Shorts under 30-90s perform best.
- **Proven home-services angles:** before/after transformations (the workhorse), oddly-satisfying tear-offs and clean-ups, "storm chaser vs. licensed local" education, drone reveals of storm damage, "5 signs you need a new roof," insurance-claim walkthroughs, tool/POV clips (magnetic nail sweep, harness/safety), crew personality/humor, and driveway-style "questions homeowners ask" explainers.
- **Hook formulas that work in the niche:** Contrarian Claim ("Your insurance company hopes you never learn this"), Mistake Warning ("Never sign this after a nor'easter"), List Tease ("3 things every LBI homeowner should check after a storm"), POV, and "this changed everything." Rotate 5-10 formulas; refresh phrasing every few months.
- **Distribution stack for NJ:** hashtag mix of #BeforeAndAfter #RoofMakeover #StormDamage + city tags (#TomsRiverNJ #JerseyShore); cross-post YouTube Shorts → Reels → TikTok (and YouTube feeds AI Overview citations); be present/responsive on Nextdoor after storms (hyperlocal trust). Storm-triggered Facebook/geo-targeted campaigns tied to a hail/nor'easter swath have produced outsized ROAS for roofers.

**Keyword architecture to hand the build team.** For paragonexteriorsnj.com (organic authority), build the pillar→service→town→blog hierarchy. Service pillars: Roofing, Siding, Windows, Doors, Gutters, Decks, Exterior Paint, HVAC, Solar, Flooring, Kitchen Remodel, Bathroom Remodel, Man Cave/Game Room, Full Home Renovation. Under Roofing, separate pages for roof repair, roof replacement, storm damage roofing, commercial roofing, and roof inspection — each targeting distinct intent. Town pages should cover the 38 towns but be built in waves of genuinely unique content (start with Toms River, Brick, Lakewood, Jackson, Howell, Freehold, Middletown, Point Pleasant, Manchester, Berkeley Township, Barnegat, Stafford Township, LBI, Asbury Park, Red Bank). Cost/price-range pages and "how to file a roof insurance claim in NJ"–style guides are the strongest AI-citation and top-of-funnel magnets. onedayhomeimprovements.com should stay conversion-first: fewer pages, strong CTAs, the AI photo-visualizer front-and-center, and offer/financing pages that meet the compliance bar below.

**Schema @graph skeleton.** Site-wide script = Organization + WebSite (with @id anchors). Homepage page-level = LocalBusiness/RoofingContractor (aggregateRating, sameAs, geo, areaServed=38 towns), WebPage, BreadcrumbList. Each service page = Service (provider→@id business) + WebPage + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList. Each town page = LocalBusiness (or Service with areaServed=that town) + BreadcrumbList + FAQPage. Validate every change in Rich Results Test + Schema Markup Validator; monitor Search Console Enhancements.

**Insurance-claim content is the NJ moat.** Jersey Shore homeowners face separate wind/hurricane deductibles (often 1-5% of dwelling coverage — e.g., 2% of $500k = $10,000 out of pocket), the ACV-then-RCV two-check process, supplement filing, and storm-chaser/AOB (assignment-of-benefits) scams. Content that walks a homeowner through documentation → opening the claim → adjuster meeting → supplements, with honest "real claim / marginal / no claim" framing, builds exactly the E-E-A-T and quotable answer blocks Google and AI engines reward — and directly differentiates from national players who don't do claim advocacy. Reinforce the legitimate/local positioning against out-of-state storm chasers (mention permanent local office, HIC #13VH13814500, that deductible-waiving is illegal in NJ).
